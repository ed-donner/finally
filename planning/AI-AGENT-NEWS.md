## Top Stories

### 1. Anthropic Launches 10 Financial Services AI Agents
**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-05/anthropic-unveils-ai-agents-to-field-financial-services-tasks) · [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/anthropic-wall-street-financial-services-agents-jamie-dimon/) · [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/news/artificial-intelligence/2026/anthropic-targets-financial-services-space-with-new-ai-agents/)

Anthropic released 10 preconfigured AI agents targeting financial services professionals across banking, insurance, asset management, and fintech. The agents can draft pitch decks for client meetings, review financial statements, and escalate cases for compliance review. The rollout also includes full **Microsoft 365 integration** and a new **Moody's data partnership**, deepening Anthropic's push into Wall Street alongside the launch of **Claude Opus 4.7** — its most capable model for financial work.

### 3. Google Cloud Next 2026: AI Agents, A2A Protocol & Workspace Studio
**Source:** [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/google-cloud-next-ai-agents-agentic-era)

- A redesigned developer platform with 200+ models including third-party models (Anthropic Claude)

### 4. Microsoft Agent 365 Now Generally Available
**Source:** [Microsoft Security Blog](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/05/01/microsoft-agent-365-now-generally-available-expands-capabilities-and-integrations/)

### 10. AI Agent Security: 2026's Growing Attack Surface
**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/2026-year-of-ai-assisted-attacks.html) · [Adversa AI](https://adversa.ai/blog/top-agentic-ai-security-resources-may-2026/) · [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com)

Security researchers are flagging a critical gap: organizations are deploying highly capable autonomous agents without adequate identity and access management (IAM) controls:
- **Okta research** found agents with broad permissions can expose secrets and access sensitive systems unsafely
- **US, UK, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand** cybersecurity agencies jointly issued guidance on securing autonomous AI in critical infrastructure
- Two frontier AI models were found to have cleared a **32-step cyber-attack range** in controlled research
- IAM bypasses discovered in early agent governance toolkits are being actively exploited
